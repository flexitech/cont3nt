Egypt a Minefield for Journalists and Reporters

Posted on August 14, 2013 by Barrett Holmes Pitner
Posted in News

Friend of Cont3nt.com, Shauna Dillavou, the founder and executive director at CommunityRED, wrote this important blog post about the dangers journalists face in conflict zones, especially Egypt following the death two journalists today and roughly 150 civilian deaths today.

The news today is incredibly unfortunate; Mick Deane, a 61-year-old TV cameraman for Britain’s Sky News was shot and killed while covering the violence in Cairo. Habiba Ahmed Abd Elaziz, 26, on leave from the Xpress, was shot dead near the Rabaah al-Adawiya mosque in Cairo as security forces approached a sit-in by Morsi supporters. Over 149 deaths have been reported nationwide today due to the Egyptian military’s crackdown on Morsi supporters.

To help out in such treacherous circumstances, I founded CommunityRED, which is committed to providing a digital safety net for journalists, citizen reporters, and activists in conflict zones throughout the world. We work to get this crucial information out without getting anyone killed, be they veteran journalists and their sources, or the average citizen with a smartphone.
